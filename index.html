The internet promised us the world, and then quietly handed us a carefully curated corner of it. Every platform you use has learned you — your habits, your preferences, your blind spots — and built invisible walls around what you see. The algorithm isn't neutral. It is a mirror held at a flattering angle, showing you more of what you already believe, already enjoy, already are. Most people never notice the walls. They just experience the uncanny comfort of a feed that always seems to get them. This site is an attempt to knock a hole in that wall.

When a video surfaces from a language you don't speak, from a country you've never visited, filmed by someone who has never thought about you — that is the internet as it was always supposed to feel. Joyful and overwhelming and genuinely surprising. There are billions of people out here uploading their lives: weddings, arguments, tutorials, disasters, jokes that don't translate, music that doesn't need to. Randomness is the only honest way to reach them. Hit the button enough times and you'll start to understand, in a way no documentary could teach you, that the world is vast and specific and worth paying attention to.
